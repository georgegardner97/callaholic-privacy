Privacy Policy — Callaholic

Last updated: 20 May 2026

Callaholic is built around a single principle: your data stays on your device. This policy explains what that means in practice.

What Callaholic does on your device

Callaholic helps you keep up with the daily practice of calling your fellows. To do that, the app stores:

  • Fellow tags. When you mark a contact as a "fellow," Callaholic saves only the stable contact identifier from your iPhone Contacts, plus any sobriety date, approximate years sober, or notes you choose to add.
  • Call logs. When you log a call, Callaholic saves the timestamp and the outcome you selected (Completed, Voicemail, No answer).
  • Preferences. Your indicator markers (e.g. "AA"), Newcomer / Old Timer thresholds, daily reminder time, and the Recently Met window are stored as settings on the device.

All of this lives in the app's local database (SwiftData) on your iPhone. None of it is uploaded to any server controlled by the developer.

What Callaholic reads from your iPhone

Callaholic asks for read-only access to your iPhone Contacts. The app uses this to:

  • Scan your contacts for the indicator markers you've configured (default: "AA") and suggest matches as potential fellows.
  • Fetch the display name and phone number of a tagged fellow when you tap Call, so the call can be placed.

Callaholic does not modify your iPhone Contacts. It does not add, edit, or delete entries.

When iOS asks how you want to share contacts, you can choose Full Access (Callaholic sees all your contacts) or Limited Access (Callaholic sees only the contacts you specifically share). Either works; Limited Access just means the indicator scan can only see the contacts you've explicitly shared.

What Callaholic sends off your device

Nothing. Callaholic has no backend server, no analytics SDK, no tracking, no advertising. The developer has no way to see your fellows, your call history, your sobriety dates, or anything else.

The only network requests the app makes are when you tap an external link (for example, a link to this privacy policy). Those open in your browser and are governed by the browser's own privacy behaviour.

Optional iCloud sync (not yet enabled)

Future versions may offer optional iCloud sync, so your fellow tags and call history follow you between Apple devices (e.g. iPhone and iPad). If we add this:

  • It will be opt-in, off by default.
  • The data syncs into your private CloudKit container — only your iCloud account can read it. The developer has no access.
  • You can turn it off anytime in Settings.

Until you explicitly turn it on, nothing leaves your iPhone.

Anonymity

Callaholic is built with respect for the 12th Tradition. The app never collects identifying information about you or your fellows, never publishes anything to a public profile, never asks for an account, and never shares data with third parties.

Permissions you may see

  • Contacts — required, as described above.
  • Notifications — optional, only used to fire the daily reminder you configure in Settings.

You can revoke either permission at any time in iPhone Settings → Privacy & Security.

Children

Callaholic is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone.
